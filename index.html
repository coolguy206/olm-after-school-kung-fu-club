ABOUT THE KUNG FU

Baat Ying Baat Faat
(8 animals 8 methods kung fu).
It is an external style of kung fu that is based on both northern & southern styles of kung fu. Baat Ying Baat Faat focuses on strong stances with fast footwork and intricate hand techniques. We combine complex, classic deflects and strikes with fast-flowing, aggressive footwork. Our training methods foster dynamic speed, power, and the ability to move and attack simultaneously.

Location/Facilities

On nice days the class will be held outside in the lower yard. On rainy days the class will be held inside the old club house room. Kids can expect to learn traditional kung fu techniques which include: 

  • Stances
  • hand techniques
  • Kicking techniques
  • Forms

The Age limit is 9 and up. There will be no belts or belt tests.

Selection Process

After registering parents will be notified via email by TBD DATE if their child has been selected to participate in the OLM After School Kung Fu Club.

Class Structure

We use the sun-moon salute to open and close class. Our warm-up routine is a sequence of kicking, punching, and stance training. The training portion varies according to the season, level of experience, and interests of the students. A typical class usually consists of:

  • Bowing in
  • Stretching
  • Beginning warms up
  • Quick water break
  • forms/ partner drills
  • Bowing out
