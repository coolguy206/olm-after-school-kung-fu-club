ABOUT THE KUNG FU

Baat Ying Baat Faat
(8 animals 8 methods kung fu).
It is an external style of kung fu that is based on both northern & southern styles of kung fu. Baat Ying Baat Faat focuses on strong stances with fast footwork and intricate hand techniques. We combine complex, classic deflects and strikes with fast-flowing, aggressive footwork. Our training methods foster dynamic speed, power, and the ability to move and attack simultaneously.

ABOUT THE CLASS

10 week program

OLM After School Kung Fu Club is a exciting 10-week program where your child will learn the fundamentals of traditional
kung fu.

Participants will learn essential techniques which include:

  • Stances
  • hand techniques
  • Kicking techniques
  • Partner fighting drills
  • Forms

These are designed to build strength, discipline, and confidence.

Cost

To instill commitment and dedication, the program is offered FREE of charge to OLM students enrolled in CLUBHOUSE; however, if a clubhouse student DOES NOT COMPLETE the full 10 weeks, a fee of $200 will be applied.

Non-Clubhouse students are also welcome at a cost of $200.

Location/Facilities

Classes take place after school on Tuesdays from 3:15 pm - 4:15 pm in the old clubhouse room at OLM.

Open to students in grades 4-8, this program focuses on skill development without belt tests or rankings.

Class Structure

We use the sun-moon salute to open and close class. Our warm-up routine is a sequence of kicking, punching, and stance training. The training portion varies according to the season, level of experience, and interests of the students. A typical class usually consists of:

  • Bowing in
  • Stretching
  • Beginning warms up
  • Quick water break
  • forms/ partner drills
  • Bowing out
